About Tigers

Tiger(lat. Panthera tigris) is a predatory mammal of the cat family, one of five species of the panther genus, belonging to the subfamily of big cats. The word "tiger" comes from other Greek. τίγρις, which in turn goes back to other Persian. *tigri from the root "taig" with the meaning "sharp; fast"

Countries with Small Tigers Populations

Countries in which the number of tigers is less than 100 are: North Korea, Laos, Vietnam, Myanmar and China. It is necessary to protect tigers since the total number is about 4000-6500 individuals. The largest population is the Bengal tiger, accounting for about 40% of the total tiger population.
